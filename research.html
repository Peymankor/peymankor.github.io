Research

You can find detailed information about my publications, software, and other work on my CV.

Statistical Modeling of the Microbiome

I work with Daniela Witten and Amy Willis to develop methods for proper statistical analysis of the microbiome. My current focus is the development of CORNCOB, a regression model and hypothesis testing procedure developed to address the challenges of microbiome data. Our proposal uses observed absolute abundance information to make inference about the latent microbial population. The manuscript and software will be made public soon, but in the meantime you can contact me to learn more.

I have also been working on DivNet, a method for estimating alpha and beta diversity of microbial communities when taxa interact via an ecological network. The software for implementing DivNet is available here.


Small-Area Spatio-temporal Modeling

I also work on developing spatio-temporal models for under-five mortality estimation with complex survey data with a group led by Jon Wakefield and Sam Clark. You can read more information about our group, including group members, publications, and software, on our webpage.
Check out our R package on CRAN, SUMMER, for all your spatio-temporal modelling with complex survey data needs!
